1.​Blockchain For Fighting insurance fraud 
Indiaforensic Center of Studies estimates that Insurance frauds in India costs about $6.25 billion annually. 

The sheer complexity of the modern insurance industry creates gaps in visibility that can be exploited to perpetrate fraud. Claims are shuffled from insurees to insurers and reinsurers in a slow, paperwork-driven process that has many moving parts. This creates opportunities for criminals to make multiple claims across different insurers for a single loss, or enables brokers to sell insurance policies and pocket the premiums. 
 
Storing and reconciling data sets of financial obligations, customer insights, fraud intelligence and ownership forms are the core of insurance claims operations. What if blockchain could manage shared intelligence to drive insurance claims processing efficiency, independent of fraud, thus bringing a structural change to the industry?  
 
Blockchain technology can enable better coordination between insurers to combat fraud. 

On a distributed ledger, insurers could record permanent transactions, with granular access controls to protect data security. Storing claims information on a shared ledger would help insurers collaborate and identify suspicious behavior across the ecosystem. 
 
For example, by using blockchain, insurers could create receipts at different points in the claims process resulting in an immutable, auditable record of all claims activities, which could be revisited by all parties, customers, brokers, insurers, co-insurers and reinsurers including the regulators. This could lead to lower transaction costs, lower transaction risks and trustless computation. Such an approach could help further reduce, if not entirely prevent, fraud. 
 
The teams are expected to build a BlockChain based system and it  ​must use Ethereum platform ​to prevent insurance frauds.

2.​Smart Locks
One of the major problems faced during transportation of goods is that they get tampered or sometimes unauthorized users get access to it leading to misuse and loss to the company. 
 
Incidents of theft from delivery vehicles are occurring nationwide and seem to be increasing. These incidents are causing havoc across the country’s supply chains leading to loss of hundreds of crore. 
 
In such case a ​Smart Lock (Digital Safe)​ that protects valuable assets in transit and provides access to only authorized users could be useful. These locks should also be able to detect tampering and attempts to open by unauthorized persons and at unauthorized locations. 

The admin of the system should be able to add authorized users who can have access to these locks and all the data should be stored in Cloud for future reference and ease of access by other concerned authorities whenever required. 
 
The teams are encouraged to innovate further to design and implement an IoT based system to ensure safety of goods.

5.Augmented reality​ (​AR​)
Augmented reality​ (​AR​) is an interactive experience of a real-world environment where the objects that reside in the real-world are "augmented" by computer-generated perceptual information, sometimes across multiple sensory modalities, including visual, auditory, haptic, somatosensory, and​ ​olfactory.The primary value of augmented reality is that it brings components of the digital world into a person's perception of the real world, and does so not as a simple display of data, but through the integration of immersive sensations that are perceived as natural parts of an environment. 

Virtual reality​ (​VR​) is an interactive computer-generated experience taking place within a simulated environment. It incorporates mainly auditory and visual feedback, but may also allow other types of sensory feedback. 
 
Mixing the real world with computer-generated images could change forever the way we interact with our surroundings. From driving to video games to healthcare, Augmented reality and Virtual reality could blend our perceptions and senses.
